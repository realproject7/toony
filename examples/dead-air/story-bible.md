# Story Bible — "Dead Air"

A thriller short. Wren hosts the loneliest shift on community radio — 2 AM, a
storm outside, an empty booth. Tonight an unknown caller breaks into the
broadcast and, line by line, describes the room she is sitting in: the lamp, the
rain, her breathing. By the time she understands, the ON AIR light dies and the
line stays open.

## Cast

- **Wren** — late-night radio host. Calm on air, unraveling off it. Short choppy
  auburn bob, silver headphones around the neck, oversized charcoal cardigan over
  a black tee, a small star stud earring. Muted teal-and-amber palette.
- **The Caller** — an unknown voice/figure. A shadowed hooded raincoat, face hidden,
  only a faint phone-screen glow. Desaturated noir. Never fully revealed.

## World rules

- One night, one booth, near-real time. The threat arrives through the broadcast,
  not the door — until the very end.
- The dread escalates through what the Caller *knows*, not through gore.
